## Problem Statement

Subscription merchants using tools like Stripe face a hidden revenue leak: when payments fail, the recovery workflow requires coordinating across 4+ disconnected systems — PSP dashboard, spreadsheets, Slack threads, and ticketing — to diagnose root cause, select a retry strategy, contact customers, and track outcomes. This is an **operations problem disguised as a billing problem**. Existing dunning tools (Chargebee, Stripe Revenue Recovery, Paddle) treat it as the latter and optimize retry timing. They don't touch the cross-system context (order status, customer history, support signals) that determines the right action.

The target buyer: a finance/treasury lead at a subscription SaaS or e-commerce business watching MRR graphs. Failed payments translate directly to churned revenue. Every day a failed charge isn't recovered is dollars that don't come back.

## Demand Evidence

- Founder has a specific person they could call tomorrow: finance/treasury role at a subscription business
- Pain: revenue leakage from failed payments they cannot recover fast enough
- Status quo: Stripe dashboard + spreadsheets + Slack + ticketing — a 4-context-switch investigation for every exception
- Industry data: up to 70% of failed payments are recoverable with appropriate retry logic; most companies recover far less because the coordination cost is too high
- Landscape signal: "agentic payments" is heating up (Natural raised $9.8M in 2026; Anchor building autonomous billing; banks shifting AI from chatbots to autonomous money movement) — timing is right

## Status Quo

The specific person with this pain today:
1. Monitors Stripe dashboard for decline alerts
2. Opens a spreadsheet to track failure history and note decline codes
3. Posts in Slack to ask customer success if the account is at-risk
4. Files a support ticket or sends a manual email to the customer
5. Follows up 3-5 days later to see if the retry worked
6. Loses track of the closed loop because no system owns the outcome

Cost: 2-3 hours per significant failed payment investigation. At scale (100+ failures/month), this becomes a dedicated ops role or an ops team.

## Target User & Narrowest Wedge

**Target user:** Finance/treasury lead or Head of Payments at a subscription SaaS company ($5M–$100M ARR, 500–10,000 subscribers). Gets promoted if MRR recovery rate goes up. Gets fired if chargeback ratio spikes or month-end close takes 3 weeks.

**First wedge:** Subscription payment repair — one PSP (Stripe), one closed loop (failure → classify → action → outcome). Demonstrable ROI: dollars recovered / recovery rate delta vs. manual baseline. Can be demoed in weeks, not months.

**What the first customer gets in week 1:**
- Ingest Stripe webhooks → classify every failure (soft/hard/3DS/risk) with root cause
- Playbook-driven action selection (retry timing, card updater, customer outreach, escalate)
- Graduated auto-execution: safe retries auto-execute, outreach/reroute require approval
- Recovery tracking: per-failure outcome with agent reasoning logged

**Not in the first wedge:** reconciliation, dispute automation, multi-PSP routing. These are the platform. The wedge is subscription payment repair.

## Constraints

- No acquiring license required (ops overlay, not infrastructure)
- First customer integration surface: Stripe webhooks + Stripe API + one order/CRM system
- Enterprise infosec will require: data residency options, SOC 2 readiness roadmap, API key scoping
- Must start in graduated autonomy model: safe actions auto-execute, risky ones require approval — never "auto-execute everything" on day 1
- Competitive landscape: Stripe Revenue Recovery and Chargebee Smart Dunning exist and are table stakes. The moat is cross-system context, not retry logic alone.

## Premises

1. The first live deployment must prove value on subscription payment repair before expanding to reconciliation or disputes — even though the platform vision covers all three.
2. The product is a PSP-agnostic ops overlay: sits on top of Stripe/Adyen/Cybersource, does not compete with acquiring. Owns infra optionally later.
3. Graduated autonomy by action type: safe retries auto-execute, outreach and PSP rerouting require approval, refunds/cancellations are manual. This is the enterprise trust model.
4. Cross-system context (order status, customer history, support signals) is the moat — not retry timing. Stripe can replicate retry timing; it cannot replicate data from the merchant's OMS, CRM, and support system.

## Approaches Considered

### Approach A: Stripe-Native Dunning Intelligence
One PSP, one action type (retry). Fast to demo but competes directly with Stripe Revenue Recovery. No defensible moat. Risk: Stripe deprecates the gap.

### Approach B: Cross-System Payment Repair Engine
Multi-PSP event ingestion + order/CRM context + playbook-driven action selection + graduated auto-execution + outcome tracking. 8 weeks to first paid pilot with a 2-3 person senior team; adjust timeline accordingly if solo. Moat is the cross-system context. Architecture extends to reconciliation and disputes without a rewrite.

### Approach C: Mandate-First Agentic Commerce Infrastructure
Build the authorization/intent layer first. Right direction, wrong timing for 2026 — no paying customer yet has the "AI agent paying on my behalf" problem at scale.

## Recommended Approach

**Hybrid B+C: Cross-System Payment Repair Engine with Mandate Layer baked in from day 1.**

Architecture:
- **Event Ingestion**: PSP webhooks (start Stripe-only, add Adyen/Cybersource in phase 2)
- **Context Store**: orders, customers, payment history, support signals, policy/thresholds
  - Sync model: event-driven sync into agent-owned Postgres; source system webhooks/polled APIs push updates; TTL-expired for support signals (24h)
  - Context enrichment: runs async from webhook ACK (webhook is immediately acknowledged to PSP); classifier waits up to 30 seconds for enrichment; if enrichment times out, classifier proceeds with partial context (PSP-only) and flags the case for human review
  - Secrets: per-tenant encrypted API keys stored in key vault (e.g., AWS Secrets Manager); no shared credentials across tenants
- **Failure Classifier**: maps decline codes + context → root cause + confidence score
  - Confidence floor: score < 0.60 → escalate to human approval queue with reasoning; score ≥ 0.60 → proceed to playbook selection
  - If context source is unavailable (API timeout): fall back to PSP-only classification, flag for human review, log the degraded context
  - Deduplication: webhook events are idempotent by PSP event ID; duplicate events are discarded
- **Playbook Engine**: selects action from configured playbooks per failure type
  - A **playbook** is a named set of prioritized rules: `IF [failure_type + conditions] THEN [action] WITH [params]`. Example rule: `IF decline_code IN [51, 65] AND retry_count < 3 AND payroll_day_proximity >= 0.7 THEN retry AT [next_thursday]`. Rules are configured by the merchant via the Playbook Builder UI — no code required. Playbooks are versioned and auditable.
  - `payroll_day_proximity`: normalized float 0.0–1.0. Represents proximity to typical payroll dates for the customer's billing country (e.g., 1st and 15th of month in the US). 1.0 = on/immediately after payroll day; 0.0 = furthest point. Computed by the agent's internal payroll-cycle model, seeded by country.
- **Mandate / Authorization Layer** (L3, designed in from day 1):
  - Structured mandate objects — minimal illustrative schema:
    ```json
    {
      "mandate_id": "M-2024-089",
      "principal": { "type": "merchant", "id": "acme-corp" },
      "agent": { "type": "system", "id": "payment-repair-v1" },
      "scope": {
        "actions": ["retry_soft_decline", "send_outreach", "escalate"],
        "max_retry_count": 5,
        "max_amount_usd": 10000,
        "require_3ds_acknowledgment": false,
        "validity_until": "<pilot_end_date>"
      },
      "risk_thresholds": { "step_up_above_usd": 5000 }
    }
    ```
  - Every auto-executed action references a mandate ID and validates against scope before execution
  - Intent receipts generated per action: mandate_id, context summary, decision summary, timestamp
  - Policy firewall: action is blocked if it exceeds mandate scope, regardless of playbook recommendation
  - Note: 3DS exemption requires explicit `require_3ds_acknowledgment: false` in mandate — merchant accepts liability shift; this field is required before any 3DS-bypass action executes
- **Action Runtime**: Stripe API (retry, update params), email/SMS (outreach), ticketing (escalate)
- **Audit Log**: inputs, reasoning, tool calls, outcomes, rollback points — per action
- **Human-in-the-loop UI**: approval queue, override, playbook tuning; A/B replay is phase 2 (manual side-by-side comparison of two playbook runs on the same historical failure cohort)
- **Autonomy model**: per-action type, configurable by the merchant within the playbook builder (not globally fixed). Default tier on day 1: safe retries = auto, outreach/reroute = require approval, refund/cancel = manual. Merchants can grant more autonomy action-by-action after trust is established.

UI Core (from wireframe):
- **Repair Queue**: failure list with failure type, root cause, suggested action, confidence, context
- **Per-action autonomy toggles** in sidebar (safe retries=auto, outreach=approve, reroute=approve, refund=manual)
- **Detail panel**: mandate badge, agent reasoning, action timeline, context signals
- **Stats strip**: at-risk MRR, recovery rate delta vs. baseline, mean time to repair, dollars recovered

The Mandate layer costs almost nothing to design in upfront (it's primarily a data model and auth check). It is nearly impossible to retrofit later when customers have built workflows on top of unstructured action logs. Design it now.

**Mandate Layer MVP effort estimate:** mandate data model + policy firewall check on action execution ≈ 3 engineer-days. Full intent receipts, rollback points, and mandate versioning ≈ phase 2 (1 sprint).

## Open Questions

1. **Which CRM/OMS to integrate first?** Salesforce, HubSpot, or Shopify — depends on the first customer's stack
2. **Mandate object standard**: design your own or adopt/extend an emerging standard? (x402 Foundation is building open payment standards for agentic commerce)
3. **Pricing model validation**: SaaS + usage (per automated execution) vs. value-share on recovered revenue. Need to test both with first 3 customers.
4. **"All three at once" tension**: founder wants platform breadth, but first customer needs a single working closed loop. Resolution: sell the platform vision, deploy the wedge, expand from evidence.
5. **3DS policy handling**: when auto-executing a retry without 3DS, liability shifts to the merchant. Resolved in design: mandate object requires explicit `require_3ds_acknowledgment: false` before any 3DS-bypass action executes. This is a required field — not optional.
6. **A/B replay scoping**: phase 2 feature; minimal version = manual side-by-side comparison of two playbook runs on the same historical failure cohort. Do not stub in phase 1.

## Success Criteria

For first paid pilot (8 weeks):
- Recovery rate ≥ 65% on soft declines — counting only agent-initiated recoveries (approved or auto-executed), assuming human approval latency < 4 hours; assumes ~40% manual baseline
- Mean time to repair ≤ 6 hours (vs. 3-5 day manual baseline)
- Zero actions executed outside mandate scope (verified via audit log on every auto-execution)
- Audit log complete for 100% of actions taken
- At least one mandate object configured per action type, covering the full scope used in the pilot

For first 3 customers (6 months):
- Measurable MRR recovery delta attributable to the system
- At least one customer expands from payment repair → dispute automation
- NRR > 100% (customers expand, don't churn)

## Distribution Plan

- Web service (SaaS): cloud-hosted (Fly.io or AWS); CI/CD pipeline to be specified at build start
- Integration path for each new merchant: Stripe webhook endpoint configuration → API key scoping → context source connector (Shopify/Salesforce/Zendesk webhook or polling)
- No binary distribution, no app store, no package manager required
- Enterprise sales motion: 30-day pilot with one PSP + one context source, then expand
